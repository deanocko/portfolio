Autonomous Maze Navigator

Mechatronics 2
Project Overview
In this assignment we were tasked with using a robot to autonomously navigate a maze, collect water, distinguish a fire and return to the original position. The task was completed in simulation as the subject was delivered during the pandemic.

The blue marker indicates the water location and the red marker that of the fire. The only data points our we were able to use was a distance sensor indicated by the dashed line and two other proximity sensors which activated when the robot was within 2m of the water or fire. The proximity sensors did not, however, indicate direction. Despite not being seen, the code is being run from an Arduino Uno which has a timer on the display and letters indicating the current objective of the robot. Controls on the Arduino's LCD keypad are used to initiate the mission.

To achieve full marks, the mission needed to be completed in under 3 minutes with marks being deducted for every 30 seconds until a cutoff point of 6 minutes. As shown in the above video, my implementation completed the mission in less than 35 seconds. Due to the random goal locations, my implementation consistently completed the mission within 30 seconds - 1 minute.
Technologies and Skills
