Missile Interceptor Project

Programming for Mechatronic Systems
Project Overview
In this assignment we were tasked with intercepting the maximum amount of 'bogies' within a certain time frame. We were only able to control the linear and angular velocity of our 'aircraft' and we could only obtain select measurements to achieve this.

These measurements were limited to the bearing and range of each bogy relative to our aircraft and the location of our aircraft relative to a static base station. Using this information I designed a flight controller which determined the bogie locations and instructed my aircraft on how to optimally pursue them.

The actual bogy locations are shown in the video using the green marker. My implementation displays the blue markers as the the point at which my aircraft could intercept each respective bogy. Due to predicting the interception point, my flight controller could straighten the flight paths of my aircraft which meant it could intercept more bogies in a given timeframe than a standard implementation. I was in the top three students in terms of interceptions and received bonus marks.

To further improve my design I could have implemented a graph search to more optimally decide the shortest route to intercept all bogies within the frame.
